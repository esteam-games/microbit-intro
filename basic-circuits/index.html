The AA Battery

  • + The "Bump": The raised end is Positive.
  • - The Flat: The smooth, flat end is Negative.

The 9V Battery

  • Both terminals are on the TOP!
  • + Smaller circle is Positive.
  • - Hexagon shape is Negative.

Our Hero: The Coin Cell

Tiny particles called Electrons (-) travel from the Negative (-) side, through the Copper Tape path, light up the LED, and return to the Positive (+) side!

LEDs

💡 🚪

LEDs are picky! They only like energy flowing one way.

Meet the Long Leg & Short Leg

  • 📏 Long Leg: Positive (+) side.
  • 📐 Short Leg: Negative (-) side.
Try it: If your light doesn't turn on, try flipping it around! It's like a one-way street for electrons.

One Path or Two?

🔌 🔀 💡

Series: The Single Path

Shared Energy: All lights share the same road. They have to split the battery's energy, so they are dimmer!

Parallel: The Side-by-Side

Full Power: Every light gets its own private road to the battery. They all stay nice and bright!

What did we learn? 🎓

  • 🏃 Electrons flow from Negative to Positive.
  • ⭕ Electrons travel in a loop to turn on the light.
  • 🧭 LEDs need the right direction to glow.
  • 🛣️ Parallel circuits keep our lights bright!

Safety First! 🛑

🔥 🔋

Never make a road that goes straight from Plus (+) to Minus (-) without a light bulb!

This is called a Short Circuit and it will make your battery and tape get very hot! Always make sure the energy has an LED to pass through.
