What is a Circuit?

🔄 ⚡

It's all about the loop!

A circuit is a continuous loop that allows electrons to flow from the negative (-) terminal of the battery to the positive (+) terminal, passing through a device like an LED to light it up.

The word “circuit” comes from the same word as circle. You’ll notice that it starts and ends at the same place.

Tiny particles called Electrons (-) travel from the Negative (-) side, through the Copper Tape path, light up the LED, and return to the Positive (+) side!

LEDs

💡 🚪

LEDs light up only when the electrons flow from negative to positive. Remember to connect your LEDs positive to the battery's positive.

Meet the Long Leg & Short Leg

  • 📏 Long Leg: Positive (+) side.
  • 📐 Short Leg: Negative (-) side.
Try it: If your light doesn't turn on, try flipping it around! It's like a one-way street for electrons.

One Path or Two?

🔌 🔀 💡

Series: The Single Path

One Road: All lights share the same path. If one of them breaks, no one lights up!

Parallel: The Side-by-Side

Full Power: Every light gets its own private road to the battery. They all stay nice and bright!
